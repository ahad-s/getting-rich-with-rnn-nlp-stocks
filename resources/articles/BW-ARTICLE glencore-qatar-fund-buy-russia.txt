Russia’s government said it would get 10.5 billion euros ($11.3 billion) from the sale of shares in its biggest oil producer, Rosneft PJSC, to Glencore Plc and the Qatar Investment Fund.
The two buyers will get equal parts of the stake, Rosneft’s Chief Executive Officer Igor Sechin said Wednesday in televised comments. The government is selling assets to plug its widest budget deficit in six years.
