The S&P 500 Index ended the five days 1 percent lower at 2,191.95, halting a three-week advance. Tech stocks led losses with a 2.9 percent drop -- the most since April -- while utilities and real-estate stocks that benefit from lower interest rates slid as the 10-Year Treasury yield reached the highest in more than a year. A three-week rally in small-cap shares reversed as the Russell 2000 Index dropped 2.5 percent. The Dow Jones Industrial Average added 0.1 percent as bank shares gained for the fourth straight week.The weekly loss halted a rally spurred by speculation that President-elect Donald Trump will enact policies that bolster growth in the U.S. and usher in a wave of deregulation in the financial industry. While bank stocks continued their climb, they did so at the slowest pace since the election.
